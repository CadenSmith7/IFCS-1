Welcome to the Red Planet: Exploring Mars with NASA's Rovers

For decades NASA has been exploring the mysteries of Mars, sending robotic rovers across its rockey terrain in search of answers to some of humanitys biggest questions: Is there life beyond Earth? What can Mars teach us about our own planet?

From the groundbreaking pathfinder mission in 1997 to the cutting-edge rovers that have been traversing Mars and making amazing discoveries for the human exploration.

Recent Posts

Curiosity

Curiosity is a Mars rover launched in 2011 and landed in 2012 to explore the planet's climate and geology. Its goal is to find out if Mars ever supported microbial life. It discovered organic molecules, seasonal methane changes, and signs of ancient freshwater lakes. The rover is nuclear-powered, carries 17 cameras, and uses tools like a robotic arm and laser to study rocks and soil.

Preserverance

Perseverance launched in 2020 and landed in 2021 to search for signs of ancient life on Mars and collect rock samples for future return to Earth. It landed in Jezero Crater, where it found evidence of an old river delta and gathered core samples. It also recorded high-quality video and sound and carried the Ingenuity helicopter — the first to fly on another planet. The rover includes technology to make oxygen from the Martian atmosphere and uses advanced navigation and sampling tools.

Spirit-Opportunity

Spirit and Opportunity were twin rovers sent to explore Mars’ surface and study signs of past water activity. Spirit landed in 2004 and lasted over six years, while Opportunity operated for nearly 15 years. Both found minerals and rock formations shaped by water, providing strong evidence that Mars once had conditions suitable for life. They were solar-powered, had robotic arms, and were packed with cameras and science tools.
